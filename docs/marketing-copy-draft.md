## How it works (5 sekcji)

### 1. Click anywhere
> You're using Slack normally. Mouse to "Compose" — like always.

### 2. SFlow watches
> Reads accessibility metadata (same data screen readers use). Privacy:
> never reads window content.

### 3. Toast appears
> 1.5 seconds after click: a small pill near cursor. "⌘N — New Message".

### 4. You learn by accident
> Tenth time you see ⌘N in context, you'll just press it. No drill.
> No flashcards.

### 5. SFlow quiets itself
> Once you've mastered a shortcut, you start typing it. SFlow stops
> showing the toast. **The app self-mutes** as you grow.
